# Planetary Psychology™ Pilot Study v0.1

## Objective

Develop a small-scale observational study to test whether environmental and planetary variables correlate with self-reported psychological states.

---

# Study Design

Type:

Observational longitudinal study

Duration:

90 Days

Participants:

10–100 volunteers

---

# Human Variables

Participants record daily:

- Mood (1–10)
- Stress (1–10)
- Energy (1–10)
- Sleep hours
- Notes

---

# Environmental Variables

Collected automatically when possible:

- AQI
- Temperature
- Humidity
- Atmospheric pressure

---

# Planetary Variables

Collected automatically when possible:

- Lunar phase
- Solar activity
- Geomagnetic index
- Day length

---

# Geographic Variables

Collected once:

- Latitude
- Longitude
- Elevation
- Urban/rural classification

---

# Example Research Questions

## PP-H001

Does AQI correlate with mood?

## PP-H002

Does AQI correlate with sleep quality?

## PP-H003

Do seasonal changes correlate with mood variation?

## PP-H004

Do lunar phases correlate with sleep duration?

---

---

# Statistical Approach

Initial analyses:

- Correlation analysis
- Time-series analysis
- Regression analysis

Results should be treated as exploratory until independently replicated.

---

# Expected Outcome

The purpose of the pilot study is not to prove the Planetary Psychology™ framework.

The purpose is to identify measurable relationships worthy of further investigation.
