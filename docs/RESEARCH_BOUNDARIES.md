# Research Boundaries

## Purpose

Planetary Psychology™ is an interdisciplinary research framework developed by Kara Sypen.

The purpose of this document is to clearly distinguish between:

- Established scientific knowledge
- Emerging research areas
- Testable hypotheses
- Philosophical interpretations

This distinction helps ensure that research conducted under the Planetary Psychology™ framework remains transparent, reproducible, and intellectually rigorous.

---

# Category 1: Established Scientific Foundations

The following areas are supported by substantial scientific evidence.

## Psychology

- Cognitive psychology
- Behavioral psychology
- Social psychology
- Personality psychology
- Developmental psychology

## Environmental Psychology

Research examining relationships between:

- Natural environments
- Urban environments
- Light exposure
- Noise exposure
- Environmental stressors

and human psychological outcomes.

## Circadian Biology

Research demonstrating relationships between:

- Sleep cycles
- Daylight exposure
- Hormonal regulation
- Cognitive performance
- Mood regulation

## Air Quality and Health

Research examining effects of:

- Air pollution
- Particulate matter
- Atmospheric conditions

on human health and cognition.

---

# Category 2: Active Research Areas

The following topics have scientific literature but remain areas of active investigation.

## Geomagnetic Activity

Questions include:

- Effects of geomagnetic fluctuations
- Possible biological sensitivity to magnetic fields
- Population-level behavioral correlations

Current evidence is mixed and requires further study.

## Elemental and Nutritional Influences

Research examining:

- Mineral availability
- Nutritional deficiencies
- Environmental chemistry

and relationships to cognition, mood, and health.

## Collective Behavioral Dynamics

Research examining:

- Social contagion
- Memetics
- Information ecosystems
- Emergent collective behavior

---

# Category 3: Testable Planetary Psychology Hypotheses

The following hypotheses are proposed for investigation.

No hypothesis should be considered established without supporting evidence.

## AQI-Mood Hypothesis

Question:

Do air quality conditions correlate with changes in mood, stress, sleep quality, or cognitive performance?

## Planetary Cycle Hypothesis

Question:

Do measurable planetary variables correlate with psychological or behavioral variables?

Examples:

- Lunar phase
- Solar activity
- Seasonal cycles
- Geomagnetic indices

## Elemental Psychology Hypothesis

Question:

Do regional elemental and mineral environments influence population-level psychological outcomes?

Examples:

- Water chemistry
- Soil composition
- Atmospheric particulates

## Antipodal Systems Hypothesis

Question:

Do antipodal locations exhibit measurable environmental or behavioral relationships beyond chance expectations?

---

# Category 4: Exploratory Concepts

These concepts may inspire research but currently lack sufficient evidence for scientific acceptance.

## Planetary Consciousness

The idea that Earth may possess emergent properties analogous to a collective mind or consciousness.

## Planetary Memory

The idea that environmental systems may preserve information beyond currently understood mechanisms.

## Resonance-Based Collective States

The hypothesis that large populations may synchronize psychologically through mechanisms not yet fully understood.

These ideas should be treated as speculative until supported by empirical evidence.

---

# Category 5: Philosophical Interpretation

Planetary Psychology™ also allows space for philosophical inquiry.

Examples include:

- Humanity's relationship to Earth
- Humanity's relationship to the cosmos
- The meaning of planetary interconnectedness
- The role of consciousness in complex systems

Philosophical interpretations should not be presented as established scientific conclusions.

---

# Scientific Commitment

Planetary Psychology™ encourages:

- Open inquiry
- Reproducibility
- Data transparency
- Statistical rigor
- Constructive skepticism
- Interdisciplinary collaboration

The framework is intended to evolve through evidence, critique, experimentation, and peer review.
